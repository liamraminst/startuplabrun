Start Running Without Getting Hurt

A sporty beginner guide to warming up, building mileage safely, choosing shoes, recovering properly, and preventing common running injuries.

Running Injury Prevention Basics

Warm Up First

Begin with 3–5 minutes of walking or light jogging, then add dynamic movements like leg swings, high knees, and walking lunges.

Increase Slowly

Do not suddenly double your mileage. A safe rule is to increase weekly distance by no more than about 10%.

Use Proper Shoes

Running shoes help absorb impact. Replace worn-out shoes when they lose support or cushioning.

Recover Hard

Rest days, sleep, hydration, and easy runs help your body adapt instead of breaking down.

Beginner Weekly Running Plan

This plan is for someone just getting into running. The goal is consistency, not speed.

Week 1–2

Run/walk 3 days per week. Example: 1 minute jogging, 2 minutes walking, repeat 8 times.

Week 3–4

Increase jogging time slowly. Example: 2 minutes jogging, 2 minutes walking, repeat 7 times.

Week 5–6

Try longer easy runs. Example: 5 minutes jogging, 2 minutes walking, repeat 4 times.

Week 7+

Build toward continuous running, but keep most runs easy and conversational.

Common Running Injuries

Shin Splints

Often caused by doing too much too soon, poor shoes, or hard surfaces.

Prevention: slow mileage increase, better shoes, rest days.

Runner’s Knee

Pain around the kneecap, often linked to weak hips, tight muscles, or overuse.

Prevention: hip/glute strength, gradual training, avoid sudden hills.

Achilles Pain

Pain near the back of the ankle, often from tight calves or sudden speed work.

Prevention: calf raises, easy warmups, avoid sudden sprinting.

Plantar Fasciitis

Heel or arch pain that can be worse in the morning.

Prevention: supportive shoes, foot mobility, calf stretching.
