Run Atlanta Strong

A beginner-friendly Atlanta running guide built for the BeltLine, Piedmont Park, Midtown hills, hot weather, smart shoes, and injury prevention.

Running Injury Prevention Basics

Warm Up First

Start with walking or light jogging, then add leg swings, high knees, and lunges.

Increase Slowly

Avoid doing too much too soon. Keep mileage increases controlled each week.

Use Proper Shoes

Choose shoes based on your foot type, comfort, support, and running surface.

Recover Properly

Rest days, sleep, hydration, and easy runs help your body adapt safely.

Atlanta Running Scene

Atlanta BeltLine

Great for beginner runs, but it gets crowded. Keep your pace controlled and watch for bikes.

Midtown & Georgia Tech

Beginner Weekly Running Plan

The goal is consistency, not speed. Start slow and build gradually.

Week 1–2

Run/walk 3 days per week. Try 1 minute jogging, 2 minutes walking, repeated 8 times.

Week 3–4

Increase jogging time slowly. Try 2 minutes jogging, 2 minutes walking, repeated 7 times.

Week 5–6

Try longer easy runs. Run 5 minutes, walk 2 minutes, and repeat 4 times.

Week 7+

Build toward continuous running, but keep most runs easy and conversational.

Common Running Injuries

Shin Splints

Usually caused by doing too much too soon, poor shoes, or hard surfaces.

Prevention: slow mileage increase, better shoes, rest days.

Runner’s Knee

Pain around the kneecap, often linked to weak hips, tight muscles, or overuse.

Prevention: hip strength, gradual training, avoid sudden hill increases.

Achilles Pain

Pain near the back of the ankle, often from tight calves or sudden speed work.

Prevention: calf raises, easy warmups, avoid sudden sprinting.

Plantar Fasciitis

Heel or arch pain that can be worse in the morning.

Prevention: supportive shoes, foot mobility, calf stretching.
